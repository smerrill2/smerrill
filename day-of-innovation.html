01 Practical Tools for Growing Your Business 25 Slides +

The foundation session. Demystified AI for a room of business owners who'd never touched it — starting with "what is AI really?" and building up to concrete tools they could implement the same week.

Why AI matters now, how the world is shifting, and why small businesses can't afford to get left behind.

02 Prompting in the Real World 35 Slides +

The deepest session. Taught 200 business owners how to actually communicate with AI — from zero-shot basics to the SCOSTARS framework. Included live exercises where attendees wrote their own prompts for their real businesses.

Prompting 101
Zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought, and reasoning prompts — explained with real comparisons side by side.
SCOSTARS Framework
A structured prompting method: Style, Context, Objective, Scale, Time, Actor, Resources — taught through a live marketing email exercise.
Live Prompt Writing
Attendees wrote prompts for their own businesses — marketing, scheduling, vendor analysis — and saw results in real time.
AI Image Generation
Covered free and paid tools (ImageFX, Microsoft Designer, Canva, Sora, MidJourney) with live prompt-to-image demos.

Zero-Shot Few-Shot Chain-of-Thought SCOSTARS Image Prompting

03 AI Empowerment Series — Safety, Marketing & the Human Element 16 Slides +

The closing session. Covered the full spectrum — from AI-powered marketing campaigns to cybersecurity threats like deepfakes and phishing. Ended with the most important message: AI is a tool, not a replacement. Human-in-the-loop is the future.

Marketing in Minutes
Audience analysis, competitive research, content creation, and campaign management — all powered by AI tools.
Cybersecurity Essentials
Social engineering, data breaches, ransomware, business email compromise — and how to defend against them.
Deepfake Awareness
What they are, how to detect them (visual + audio analysis), and prevention strategies for business owners.
Human-in-the-Loop
The closing thesis — AI should enhance human decision-making, not replace it. Ethical considerations and continuous learning.
